Composite

Learning the Basics

The final project that we did in the class was creating a photo composite. The first task to the project was to follow a tutorial for a composite image. It taught us lightning, using layer masks, painting different parts of and image and many shortcuts. This really helped us get used to photoshop and have a good feeling when going into the actual project. After we followed the tutorial we had to think of a composite image that we could create for the final project. The image that I decided to create was to have an island in a bottle.

Composite Building Blocks

After I had the idea of what I wanted to create I began to look for images I could use in my project, but one of the criterias of the project was to have atleast one of the photos to be one that I took on my own. I decided to have the picutre of the bottle to be the one I took and the rest to be photos from various sources. When taking the photo I used paper as a white background and many lamps to reduce the shadows.

Photo Composite Final

After I took the photo I had to remove the background of the original image. I then cropped out an island so I could use it in my project and cropped out a mountain to use as the lower part of the island. I put the island and the lower island in the bottle, and changed the layer type of the bottle for it to be see through, this allowed the picture to look more realisitc and belivable. Next I used a layer mask to only have the parts in the bottle visible for the water. I changed various layer's brightness, contrast and levels to make them blend better together. Finally I found the background image and alligned it with the water level of my bottle.
